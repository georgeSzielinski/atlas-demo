# Forecasting AI Survey

This survey compares candidate forecasting models for future Atlas research.
It is documentation only. No production engine changes are implied.

## Summary Recommendation

Start with an offline benchmark harness before integrating any model. Use the
same Atlas OHLCV slices, horizons, and validation metrics for all candidates.
Kronos should be the first finance-specific candidate to benchmark because its
input contract directly matches candlestick data. Chronos and TimesFM should be
the strongest general-purpose baselines.

## Atlas Research Ranking

1. Kronos: first finance-specific OHLCV benchmark.
2. Amazon Chronos: first general-purpose probabilistic benchmark.
3. Google TimesFM: long-context general benchmark.
4. Lag-Llama: probabilistic confidence-calibration benchmark.
5. Moirai: broader Uni2TS/GluonTS research candidate.
6. PatchTST: trainable architecture baseline.

## Next Research Questions

- Which horizon should Atlas standardize first: 5 trading days, 10 trading days, or 20 trading days?
- Should benchmarks forecast close-only, OHLCV, or action-level direction directly?
- What minimum history length is acceptable for approved ETFs and stocks?
- Should Atlas rank models by hit rate, average return, calibration, or a blended score?
- How should probabilistic outputs map into Atlas `confidence`, `expected_change`, and `forecast_score`?
